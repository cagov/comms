Please test this work in progress!

It’s been a very busy, very productive six weeks for our team. (Even despite the holiday season!)

As we’ve documented in our weekly “Done/doing” blogs, we’ve begun to build the proverbial plane, even as we’ve approaching cruising altitude.

To date, we have: configured our instances of Github, GSuite and Slack - tools not widely used in California state government. Gotten our cloud environment up and running ( thanks to a huge lift from CDT). Established initial workplace norms, ceremonies and workflows, design and operational principles. Bootstrapped a web design architecture, developed and tested prototypes (including this new blog, which is open source and 100% accessible). Said “Hello California!” Begun the ongoing process of gathering, researching and prioritizing user needs. Pushed out our first user survey. Gotten a sample SMS text up and running. Collaborated with, learned from, and greatly appreciated working with our state partners in our research, testing and fact checking.

The ramp up was chaotic at times, like any start up. We’ve hit plenty of speed bumps along the way - and have plenty of learnings to share:

Establishing a culture of collaboration and actually getting to know each other as people, from the outset, are among the most important things we’ve done. And while we’re still learning how to work together and trying to figure out the simple things like finding the right temperature for the office (a work in progress), we have a solid foundation for navigating and resolving conflict.

We can’t emphasize enough how critical Sprint 0, which was all about planning and logistics, was before we could kick-off. Finding and setting up the office, hiring staff, figuring out the right number of chairs we actually need, working with Legal to vet tools and other basic operational needs are a huge undertaking and, often, underestimated.

Launching an alpha just before the winter holidays is not an optimal timeline. Doing the kick-off during the holidays has meant that roles and responsibilities have had to shift in order to keep up momentum while team members were away. This heightened what was already proving to be a challenge around operationalizing our workflows to figure who’s doing what, when, and what the hand off of tasks between teams members looks like.

Working in the agile way has also been a challenge. It’s fundamentally different from how state government typically operates - and it’s a new way of working for most of us (see building the plane metaphor above). At the same time, many of us are learning new tools most notably GitHub, which we’re using as our primary workflow management tool.

Another significant challenge has been scaling our user testing. User research is a newer discipline for the State. The status quo depends on operationalizing untested assumptions of what we think people need. We are trying to set a new way of work in this alpha, but as a result, none of the basics were in place when we got started. For example, there was no legal template for participant consent for qualitative user interviews. And we still don’t have the ability to compensate people for the time they spend talking to us and sharing their stories (we’re working on it).

Other minor but impactful challenges have slowed our role at points. Things like having WiFi before our electricity was turned on, working around partner’s availability for fact-checking user stories, and procuring new tools and subscriptions (always takes longer than anticipated).

We’ll be posting more about our challenges and learnings in the weeks ahead. But the most important learning we can offer at this point is: when it comes to creating user-focused digital services, there is no such thing as “done,” or “final.” There are always iterations and improvements that can be made.

It’s in that spirit that we share our first product release with you.

These eight new prototypes - a homepage, find your minimum wage, request a birth certificate, find official California state holidays, hire a licensed contractor for home improvements, find food banks near you, and news (this blog post) - are a work in progress. They’re iterations on where we started. They’ve been user tested (to the extent possible) and improved based on that feedback and will continue to be tested and iterated on. They’re not meant to be perfect, and we need your feedback to help make them better.

The prototypes are as accessible as possible (each scores 100 on Lighthouse). And none exceed a 6th grade reading level. In these critical ways, they provide simpler, clearer, faster access to state government information and services.

We hope you’ll give us feedback so that we can continue to learn, test, iterate and improve these pages.

Below, we’ve tried to capture a before/after for each user story, as well as some of the user feedback we got testing the prototypes. A complete account of each user story can be found on GitHub.
